FinTech — Dashboard — 2026

Ledger, a reconciliation tool

A legacy, spreadsheet-driven back-office process rebuilt as a fast, keyboard-first dashboard for a finance team reconciling thousands of transactions a day.

LEDGER — OVERVIEW

§ A — The brief

The problem

The finance team was reconciling transactions across three disconnected spreadsheets, with matching done by hand. Errors were common, and closing the books took most of a week each month.

The brief: a single tool that surfaces mismatches automatically, and that a keyboard-first user can move through without touching a mouse.

§ B — The approach

What I built

A dense, information-first dashboard with a command-palette-style navigation layer, virtualized tables for large transaction sets, and an automatic matching engine with a manual override queue for edge cases.

Design and build happened in parallel — every layout decision was tested against real transaction data from week one.

§ C — Outcome

Results

TIME TO CLOSE

5 days → 4 hours

Monthly reconciliation went from most of a week to under a business day.

MATCH ACCURACY

99.2%

Automatic matching now handles the vast majority of transactions without manual review.

TEAM ADOPTION

100% in week one

The whole finance team moved off spreadsheets in the first week after launch.
