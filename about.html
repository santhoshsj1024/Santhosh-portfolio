Fig. 01 — About

A developer's precision, a designer's eye.

I've spent eight years moving between design and engineering roles, which means I don't hand off a Figma file and hope for the best — I build the thing I designed, and I design with the constraints of the build in mind.

§ A — Background

How I got here

I started as a print designer, moved into front-end development to build the sites I was designing, and never really left either discipline behind. That dual background shapes how I work now: every design decision gets pressure-tested against real performance and accessibility constraints before it ships.

Today I work directly with founders, product teams, and agencies who need someone who can own a project from wireframe to deployed, production-ready code.

§ B — Approach

How I work

Every project starts with the same question: what is this page's single job? From there, I build a small system — color, type, layout, one signature idea — rather than a stack of one-off decisions. It keeps the result coherent and makes it fast to extend later.

I test in the browser early and often. A design that only works in a static mockup isn't finished.
